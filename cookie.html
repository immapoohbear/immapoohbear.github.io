Cookie Policy

A cookie is a small text file stored by a web server that websites place on the computers and mobile devices of people who visit those websites. It can be read and retrieved only by the server which created the cookie. These text files allow a website to remember your device and how you interacted with the website, which is useful for a number of different purposes.

WeNetwork stores cookies on computers or mobile devices for the purpose of improving the user experience.

Performance cookies track how frequently you visit our websites and how you interact with them. This information is used to have a better understanding of how our readers interact with our news and to enhance our sites and applications so that users have a better experience. For example, we gather data on which of our sites are most often viewed and by what categories of people. Third-party cookies are also used to improve speed.

Advertising cookies are used to gather information about your visit to our site, such as the content you viewed, the links you followed.

You can manage or delete cookies at your own preference. Visit AllAboutCookies for more details.

You may erase all cookies from your computer, and most modern web browsers allow you to prevent cookies. However, disabling cookies would need to change your user settings each time you access our website.

Do Not Track

Many web browsers have a Do Not Track feature that allows users to send Do Not Track requests to websites. At the moment, major Internet standardization organizations have not set regulations defining how websites should accept these requests. If you enable Do Not Track in your browser, WeNetwork LLC websites will honor your choice.
